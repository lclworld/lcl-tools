Custom vs. Private Label
"What's the difference?" "Which one should I choose?"
Great question. Here's a quick breakdown: CUSTOM FORMULATION — Developed from scratch, specifically for your brand — Full control over ingredients and performance profile — Longer timeline; requires testing and iteration — Best for brands with a specific vision or differentiated positioning PRIVATE LABEL — Existing, tested formulation produced under your brand name — Faster to market; lower development cost — Less customisation, but still carries your label — Best for brands starting out or testing the market If you're unsure, a consultation can help clarify the best route.

FDA Registration — What We Do & Don't Do
"Can you register my product?" "Do I need FDA registration?" "Will I get an INCI list?"
We do not handle FDA registration on behalf of clients — this is done by the brand owner directly, as you are the legal registrant of your product. What we offer is FDA registration guidance: we walk you through the process, explain what's required, and ensure your product documentation is in order so you can register with confidence. All formulations we develop come with the relevant product documentation, including the INCI ingredient list — required for labelling and regulatory purposes. Whether you need registration depends on where you plan to sell. If you're targeting regulated markets, this should be planned from the start of your product development journey.
Volume vs. Weight (ml is not equal to g)
"A 100ml bottle holds 100g right?" "Why doesn't weight match the volume?"
Volume (ml) and weight (g) are not the same thing. A 100ml bottle does not automatically hold 100g of product. The actual weight depends on the product's density, which varies by formulation. A water-based serum may be close to 1g/ml; an oil-heavy or thick cream will differ. This is why we confirm fill weights during the formulation stage — not from container volume alone. We'll provide the correct fill weight for your specific formula before you finalise any packaging decisions.
Natural / Organic Claims & Preservatives
"I want a 100% natural formula" "I don't want preservatives" "How long will it last?"
NATURAL / ORGANIC CLAIMS: We can formulate with natural and plant-derived ingredients. However, "100% natural" and "organic" are regulated claims — using them without proper certification is a compliance risk. We'll help you formulate a product that meets your ingredient philosophy and advise on honest labelling. PRESERVATIVES: For any water-containing formulation (creams, lotions, gels, emulsions), a preservation system is not optional — it is a safety requirement. Unpreserved water-based products are a microbial risk to the end user. SHELF LIFE: Determined during the development process, depending on formulation type, packaging, and storage conditions. We do not compromise on safety in formulation.
